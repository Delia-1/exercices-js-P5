The Rolling Stones

The Rolling Stones are a legendary British rock band formed in London in 1962. Originally led by frontman Mick Jagger and guitarist Keith Richards, the band became one of the most influential and enduring acts in rock history. Known for their rebellious image and energetic performances, The Rolling Stones helped define the sound of rock and roll with iconic hits like “(I Can’t Get No) Satisfaction,” “Paint It Black,” and “Sympathy for the Devil.” Their music blends blues, rock, and R&B influences, and they have maintained a massive global following for over six decades.
